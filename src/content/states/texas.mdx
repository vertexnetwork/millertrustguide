commonDenialReasons:
  - title: "No income deposited into the QIT account in the initial month"
    description: "HHSC disregards income for Medicaid eligibility purposes the first month a valid QIT is signed, the QIT account is set up, and enough income is deposited into the account to reduce remaining countable income below the special income limit. A partial deposit in the initial month is allowed and will not invalidate the QIT, but no deposit at all means no income disregard that month."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
  - title: "Partial source diversion in any month after the initial month"
    description: "Once a source of income is named in the QIT, the entire amount of that source must be deposited into the QIT account every month after the initial month. Depositing only part of a listed source in a non-initial month invalidates the QIT."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
  - title: "Trust not irrevocable, or revocable through court action"
    description: "Per CMS, a trust that says it is irrevocable but allows revocation through court action does not meet the irrevocability requirement. HHSC follows the CMS position."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
  - title: "Missing or defective reversion (residuary) clause"
    description: "The QIT instrument must name the State of Texas as the residuary beneficiary of any funds remaining at the beneficiary's death, up to the total amount of Medicaid assistance provided. A missing or defective reversion clause is a denial cause."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
  - title: "Resources deposited into the QIT account"
    description: "The QIT account may only receive the beneficiary's income from sources listed in the trust. Depositing resources (savings, gifts from family, etc.) into the QIT account can cause the entire account to become a countable resource, which may put the applicant over the $2,000 resource limit."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
  - title: "Trustee fails to distribute by end of the following month"
    description: "Income deposited into the QIT account must be distributed by the last day of the following month. Per HHSC, failure to timely distribute is treated as a transfer of assets, which can trigger a Medicaid penalty period."
    agencyCitation: "TX HHSC Appendix XXXVI (Revision 26-1)"
