# Cover copy — Miller Trust Basics (v1.0)

This document is the *copy* for the cover. Visual design (layout, typography, exact color values, image elements) is a separate brief — pass this text to the cover designer along with the brand guidance below.

---

## Brand visual guidance for the designer

**Aesthetic target.** Reads as a serious operational reference work, in the lineage of Nolo press, AARP printed guides, and Mayo Clinic patient publications. Not self-published genre fiction. Not SaaS startup. The buyer demographic is a 54-year-old caregiver daughter who is comparing the book mentally to her copy of *The Nolo Guide to Social Security and Medicare* — that's the bar.

**Palette.**
- Primary: deep teal `#115E59`
- Title type and accent rules: deep teal
- Body type: dark gray `#1F2937`
- Cover background: warm off-white `#FAFAF7` (NOT pure white)
- Accent (sparingly): warm rust `#B45309`

**Typography.**
- Headings (title, subtitle, byline): **Source Serif Pro** Bold, or close substitute (Charter, Iowan Old Style, Georgia)
- Body / blurb text: **Inter** Regular and Medium, or close substitute (system sans-serif acceptable: -apple-system, Segoe UI)

**Forbidden visual moves.**
- No stock photography of seniors. No grandma hugs, no family-at-the-beach.
- No gradients, no glassmorphism, no glow effects.
- No purple, pink, or neon greens.
- No AI-generated illustration that looks AI-generated.
- No "DUMMIES" yellow-and-black ribbon styling.
- No script fonts.

**Required visual elements.**
- The "MT" mark (teal square, white serif "MT") used on the site and the kit PDF. The cover designer can downscale or restyle but should keep recognizable continuity.
- The teal accent rule at the top of the cover (mirrors the kit cover's top accent).
- Small `MILLERTRUSTGUIDE.COM` wordmark on the back cover.

Twenty-four states cap Medicaid long-term-care eligibility at $2,982 per month
of income (2026). Most middle-income retirees fall above the cap on Social Security
plus a modest pension. Nursing-home care averages $7,500 to $11,000 per month.
The income gap that opens between those two numbers is the most common reason
families pay out of pocket for months — sometimes years — before Medicaid kicks in.

The Qualified Income Trust, often called a Miller Trust, is the federal-statute
mechanism that closes the gap. Properly set up, it diverts the excess income into
an irrevocable trust that Medicaid doesn't count, qualifying the applicant for
coverage. This book is the plain-English introduction — what a Miller Trust is,
how the federal statute behind it works, what the trustee does each month, and
what commonly goes wrong.

## SPINE

### Special note on spine feasibility

The ebook version has no spine.

The print-on-demand paperback version's spine width depends on final page count and paper weight. Based on the manuscript length (~17,000 words, expected 90–110 print pages at KDP's standard 6×9 trim with default formatting), the spine will be approximately **0.20–0.25 inches** at white paper, or slightly less at cream paper.

KDP's policy: **spine text is not recommended for spines under 0.0625 inches; spine text is permitted but tight under 0.25 inches.** A 90–110-page book is at the lower margin of where spine text reads cleanly. Two options:

**Option A — no spine text.** Use a solid teal spine, MT mark centered. Simplest, most reliable.

**Option B — minimal spine text (only if final page count yields ≥ 0.20" spine).** Title only, no subtitle, no author. Type rotated 90°.

### Copy if Option B is chosen

Read top-to-bottom (US convention) along the spine, rotated 90° clockwise from the front cover orientation.

```
MILLER TRUST BASICS    JAMES WHITFIELD    [MT mark]
```

Font: Source Serif Pro Semibold, size to fit 70% of spine width.

## ISBN / barcode requirements

KDP provides a free ISBN for ebooks (Amazon-assigned ASIN replaces ISBN for Kindle; KDP Print can use a KDP-issued ISBN or a publisher-purchased one).

For maximum distribution flexibility (Apple Books, Google Play, Kobo, IngramSpark for wider paperback distribution), purchase an ISBN from Bowker (US) at $125 for one, $295 for ten. Use a different ISBN for the ebook and the paperback.

The barcode area on the back cover should be at least 2" × 1.2" and placed in the bottom-right corner of the back cover. KDP's cover template includes the barcode area marked.

## Production checklist (designer hand-off)

When the cover design is finalized, deliver:

1. **Front cover only (ebook):** `cover-front-ebook.jpg`, 1600 × 2560 px, RGB, 72 dpi. Used for KDP/Apple/Google/Kobo upload.
2. **Full wrap cover (paperback):** `cover-wrap-print.pdf`, sized to KDP's downloaded template for the final trim + page count + paper, CMYK, 300 dpi, with bleed and crop marks.
3. **Source files:** the editable design file (Affinity Publisher, InDesign, or Figma export with embedded fonts).
4. **Color reference:** confirmed color values for the teal — `#115E59` on screen, Pantone 7715 C as closest PMS match for print color matching.
5. **License confirmation** for any fonts used in the design (Source Serif Pro and Inter are both open-source / SIL-licensed, free for commercial use; confirm any substitutes are also commercially licensed).
